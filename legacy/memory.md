## Mutualism interpretation caveat (Apr 27, 2026)

- Deterministic role asymmetry from parameter asymmetry (`b1 > b0`) should be distinguished from stochastic symmetry breaking.
- In Hamilton-style symmetric settings (`b1 = b0`), strong cooperation/fitness asymmetry can still emerge, but role identity is random across runs.
- In mutualism near the diagonal (`b1 ~ b0`), expect a mixed regime: deterministic bias plus stochastic role assignment.
- Current mutualism aggregate summaries may hide run-level random asymmetry near the diagonal because they average across populations/runs.
- Planned follow-up: generate mutualism `1run` for all cells and test how role-locking probability changes with `Delta x = (b1-c1) - (b0-c0)`.
