### <strong>Fig. 2.</strong> Outcome asymmetry under parameter-symmetric cooperation cost (partner choice).

![fig2](/home/marcelino/figures/interpretation/fig2.png)

<strong>Fig. 2.</strong> Short-memory partner choice. Frequency of cooperators and average fitness when short-memory partner choice is the only cooperation mechanism allowed. The figure shows two populations (red for the population with the lower frequency of cooperators, orange for the other). (<strong>a, b</strong>) Prisoner's dilemma. (<strong>c, d</strong>) Snowdrift. The two populations have the same cooperation cost (<em>c</em>, ranging from 0 to <em>b</em>, the benefit to the partner) and the same information cost (0.001).

In snowdrift, a similar split already appears without enforcement machinery; partner choice mainly reshapes outcomes at high cooperation cost. Compare Fig. 3 and the line-slice contrast in Fig. S4.
