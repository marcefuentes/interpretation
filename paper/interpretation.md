### **Fig. 2.** Outcome asymmetry under parameter-symmetric cooperation cost (partner choice).

![fig2](/home/marcelino/figures/interpretation/fig2.png)

**Fig. 2.** Short-memory partner choice. Frequency of cooperators and average fitness when short-memory partner choice is the only cooperation mechanism allowed. The figure shows a two populations (red for the population with the lower frequency of cooperators, orange for the other). (**a, b**) prisoner's dilemma. (**c, d**) snowdrift. The two populations have the same cooperation cost (*c*, ranging from 0 to *b*, the benefit to the partner) and the same information cost (0.001). Two populations with identical cooperation cost (c0 = c1) and information cost under partner choice. In the prisoner's dilemma (top row), partner choice stochastically assigns cooperator and exploiter roles that are absent under the no-machinery control (Fig. S6); the cooperator population can earn lower fitness (paradox of success). In snowdrift (bottom row), a similar split already appears without enforcement machinery; partner choice mainly reshapes outcomes at high cooperation cost. Compare Fig. 3 and the line-slice contrast in Fig. S4.

(**a, b**) Population with the higher cooperation cost (*c**<sub>1</sub>*, from 0.12 to *b*, the benefit to the partner). (**c, d**) Population with the lower cooperation cost (0.1).
